  • gamesWelcome to McGill IEEE Student Branch

    McGill University IEEE (the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers Inc.) student branch was established in April 1968 and currently has over 260 members, one of the largest IEEE student branches in Eastern Canada. The IEEE student branch at McGill is dedicated to the development of professional skills of electrical & computer engineering as well as computer science students. IEEE is the world’s leading professional not-for-profit association for the advancement of technology. Established in 1884, IEEE currently has more than 400,000 members in 150 countries.  which surpassed 100,000 in 2010.

  • gamesOur Logo

    The McGill IEEE SB logo is a representation of Kirchhoff's current law (KCL) showing current current existing a node to be the sum of the currents going into it. This symbolizes how the strength of an organization such as IEEE is the sum of its members and groups. The shape of the logo's outline represents the iron-ring, symbol of Canadian engineering. The three colours in the photo are McGill's red, IEEE's blue and WIE's purple colour.

  • vpn_keyOur Goals and Mission

    We are a group of undergraduate and graduate electrical & computer engineering and computer science students who want to have fun, to make friend and meet new people, to learn about research and innovation in technology, to disseminate the knowledge of electrical engineering, to find ourselves and our passions, to help others to advance and become more, and to get inspired to change our community!

  • classThe Constitution
    1.0 Name and Purpose

    1.0 Name and Purpose

    This organization shall be known as the McGill IEEE Student Branch.

    The purpose of this organization shall be the dissemination of knowledge of the theory and practice of all aspects of Electrical and Electronics Engineering and related fields and the furtherance of the professional development of the students.

    The organization and operations of the branch shall be in accordance with the Constitution and Bylaws of the IEEE.

    Close
    2.0 Membership

    2.0 Membership

    Membership shall be limited to undergraduate and graduate students of McGill University, Montreal QC, who are members of the IEEE.

    Close
    3.0 The Executive Committee

    3.0 The Executive Committee

    The Executive Committee (EC) shall be the governing body of the Branch and shall transact all business of the Student Branch. This includes decisions related to direction, recruitment, member benefits, event organizations, communication with IEEE, expenditures, proposals, member discipline and administrative duties related to the Student Branch.

    The Executive Committee consists of elected officials who are members of good standing of the Student Branch, and the EC consists of Chairman, Vice Chairman, Secretary, and Treasurer.


    3.1 Eligibility for Candidates of the Executive Committee

    A candidate for any of the Executive positions must be a member of good standing of the Student Branch.

    The term of office shall ordinarily be one year.

    A candidate cannot hold an office consecutively for more than 2 terms.


    3.2 Duties of Officers

    More details can be found on the IEEE Student Concourse


    The Chairman:

    Shall preside at all meetings of the Branch. He/She shall appoint all committees, subject to the approval of the Executive Committee, and shall assume all other executive duties not otherwise delegated.


    The Vice Chairman:

    Shall perform all functions of the Chairman in his/her absence or at his/her request.


    The Secretary:

    Shall keep a record of all activities of the Branch and shall report same to IEEE Headquarters.

    Shall prepare the minutes of each meeting and deliver the minutes to the anticipated attendees and others who need to be informed, within 5 days of the meeting.

    Shall keep a record of the names and IEEE membership numbers of all members.

    At the end of each year, the Secretary shall prepare all documents required by the IEEE Manager of Student Services and deliver it to IEEE student services.


    The Treasurer:

    Shall be responsible for maintaining the Bank Account.

    Shall receive all money and pay all debts of the branch authorized by the executive committee.

    Shall keep all financial records current and in order.

    Shall provide a financial statement at the last EC meeting of each month.

    Shall submit a complete annual report at the end of the financial year, to the EC.

    Shall prepare all financial documents required for annual reports to be submitted to the IEEE Manager of Student Services, upon certification by the Counselor.


    3.3 Election of Officers The Presiding Officer:

    The Chair will appoint a Presiding Officer to organize and conduct the election of the election officers of the Executive.

    The Presiding Officer cannot be a candidate for any of the positions.

    The Presiding officer follow the directive of the Executive in advertising, calling for nominations, conducting the election, determining the successful candidates and announcing the results.

    Election of officers for the following year shall be held no later than the last meeting of each year.


    The Nominations:

    Nominations should be forwarded by email or in writing to the Presiding officer.

    All nominees must be members in good standing at the Student Branch.

    Nominations will be accepted at the Annual General Meeting or prior to the meeting.

    Candidates with previous IEEE experience are preferred for the position of Chair.


    The election:

    In the event that more than one candidate has been nominated for a position, an election may be held during the Annual General Meeting.

    4.0 Student Branch Counselor

    4.0 Student Branch Counselor

    A Student Branch Counselor who is an IEEE member and a faculty member of McGill University, teaching in an IEEE designated field is to be appointed by the EC.

    The appointment or reappointment shall normally be for two years.

    Each Branch Counselor is charged with promoting the welfare of the IEEE at the institution, particularly in matters relating to Student activities.

    Close
    5.0 Meetings

    5.0 Meetings

    The Branch shall hold regular and special meetings at such places and time as designated by the Executive Committee, with a minimum of six (6) meetings a year. The target is an Executive meeting every month. A minimum of 5 days notice is required for any meeting. An agenda should be distributed with the notice of the meeting.

    All members of the Executive are expected to attend meetings and those who are unable to attend a meeting should inform the Secretary, prior to the meeting.

    The Chair may take disciplinary action for continued non-attendance of meetings.

    Close


    6.0 Financial

    6.0 Financial

    The Student Branch shall maintain only one bank account in the name of the organization “McGill IEEE Student Branch”. The Treasurer is responsible for deposit of all funds collected into this account in an expeditious manner.

    All withdrawals from this account shall be by cheque signed by the Treasurer and the Chair. All withdrawals should have been approved by the Executive committee, and the reason for the withdrawal should be included in the minutes of the executive meeting.

    Close
    7.0 Reporting to IEEE Student Services

    7.0 Reporting to IEEE Student Services

    The Treasurer and the Secretary are responsible for compiling all the information required for the following reports that are required by the IEEE Student Services:

    Annual Report of Activities endorsed by the IEEE Counselor - Due May 1

    The Reports should be forwarded to the Manager of Student Services, the Regional Student Activities Committee Chair, the Regional Student Representative, and the Section, on or before the deadline to claim the rebates.

    Close
    8.0 Amendments

    8.0 Amendments

    The Branch is empowered to adopt Bylaws, which are consistent with this Constitution. The Constitution may be amended by a simple majority of the members of the branch. Any amendments should be publicized prior to the meeting, usually by including it in the Agenda. All amendments will be incorporated and will be effective upon the approval of the Executive Committee.
